About Me

In 1996 a friend showed me that you could save any webpage, tweak it in Notepad, and make it your own. I spent nights cloning layouts, building phpBB skins, and sketching ideas in a stack of dog-eared notebooks.

I loved art just as much, so I took a scholarship to the Columbus College of Art & Design summer program and kept sneaking back to HTML whenever I could. I knew I wanted a creative career, but I couldn’t see how to make it pay—programming sounded like cubicle life and bug fixes forever. Finding out there was a “frontend developer” path that blends design and code was the breakthrough.

Later I found writers like Lea Verou and Chris Coyier, devoured every Smashing Magazine post I could find, and spent nights poking at CodePen demos to see how people engineered those effects. That mix of art, community tips, and sheer fascination with putting pixels on a page is still what drives this blog.

It wasn’t until much later that I really dug into user experience, user flows, grids, and everything else that shapes modern sites. What hooked me—and still does—is the creativity, freedom, and community. There are always new tricks people are sharing, and AI only expands the palette. Just like you can still shoot 35mm film or post an unfiltered photo, you can keep things authentic while embracing new tools. That balance keeps the craft exciting.
