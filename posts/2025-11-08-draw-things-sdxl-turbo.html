3-Step SDXL Turbo Workflow in Draw Things

Published November 8, 2025 • Generative Art, Workflow

SDXL Turbo plus Draw Things on an M5 Max makes high-res assets feel instant. The app runs the entire stack locally, so there is no ChatGPT or cloud subscription in the mix—just a one-time model download sitting on fast local storage. With 3 inference steps and the Euler A (Substep) sampler, I can turn a scribbled idea into a publishable render before Finder can finish syncing screenshots.

Hardware baseline

The M5 Max’s neural engine is available, but Draw Things leans on the GPU cores. I keep the project folder on the internal SSD, let the app allocate 12 GB of VRAM, and disable background upscalers so Turbo can sprint. That combination keeps each generation under two seconds—even when I am juggling other creative apps.

The three steps

  1. Load SDXL Turbo: Models live under ~/Library/Application Support/Draw Things/models/. I point the project at sdxl_turbo_v1.1 and lock CFG at 1.5 to stay faithful to prompts.
  2. Set Euler A Substep: In Sampler settings choose Euler A (Substep), set Step Count to exactly 3, and toggle Turbo Mode. This keeps paths sharp while honoring Turbo’s speed advantage.
  3. Prompt, queue, publish: I keep three prompt presets (character, creature, prop). Drop in the reference, queue two seeds, and export straight to the blog’s images/originals folder so the ImageMagick script can batch-resize everything.

No loras, no ControlNet, and no extra passes—the point is to stay within three steps so ideation never stalls.

Why this matters

Because everything runs on-device, I can prototype without internet, keep client prompts private, and avoid the latency of cloud inference. SDXL Turbo loves short step counts, so pairing it with a disciplined three-step recipe keeps ideation nimble. I have stopped bouncing to paid assistants unless I need help writing copy—visual ideas live entirely inside Draw Things.
